Skill Condition Viewer

If you've ever been confused by what a particular activation condition of a skill means, this tool will help you.

The "Skill filter" button allows you to view only conditions relevant for a particular skill. You can also do this from the details pop-ups on the Skills page.

▼

How to read conditions

Every skill has one or more sets of conditions. The skill will trigger immediately when an entire set is satisfied (most skills also require a Wit roll to succeed).

Every condition has a value. For example, if the condition is ground_type==2, then "ground_type" is the name of the condition, "==" is the operator, and "2" is the value.

The operators can be:

  • == (equals)
  • != (does not equal)
  • > (greater than)
  • >= (greater than or equal)
  • < (less than)
  • <= (less than or equal)
For example, a condition accumulatetime>=5 can be read as "the number of seconds since the race has started is 5 or greater". On the other hand, accumulatetime==5 would trigger if the number of seconds was exactly five.

The conditions can be "linked" by two characters, & or @.

  • & means "and"
  • @ means "or"

Let's say that we have the following conditions:
distance_type==1
&ground_type==1
@
distance_type==2
&ground_type==1

In that case, the skill will trigger if the distance is 1 (Sprint) and the ground is 1 (Turf). OR, alternatively, the skill will also trigger if the distance is 2 (Mile) and the ground is 1 (Turf).

If a skill has multiple condition sets (triggers), they will be checked from top to bottom. If Trigger 1 procs, Trigger 2 won't proc anymore. The exception to this are skills with the is_activate_other_skill_detail condition, where Trigger 2 becomes a "continuation" of Trigger 1, and both can happen in one race.
